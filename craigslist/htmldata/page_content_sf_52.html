'73 Fiat 500 Classic "Cinquecento"

I imported this car years ago and it was displayed in my collection. I'm downsizing and selling off some toys, and this great little car is now available.

It runs like a sewing machine and everything works (lights, horn, signals, etc.). Really a nice solid unrestored car. There are some cosmetic issues such as scratches, dings, and minor rust bubbles under the paint in a few isolated areas. Enough flaws to give it a little character, but still looks very presentable. Nothing has been redone or covered up so you can see what you're getting - Why pay $15k plus for one of these with a fresh shiny paint job that covers questionable bodywork and other unseen issues?

Very nice original interior in black with red carpet accent. Black sunroof also. This car came from Florence, and the (Firenze) emissions decal is still in place on the windshield. This car also has the deluxe chrome bumper over-rider bars and interior upgrades.

California title in my name. No back fees.

It may seem romantic to dream about buying one of these in Italy and shipping it back yourself, but it can become an expensive nightmare to actually do this. There is also not much of a gap between prices there and here, so it would be very easy to get in "over your head" when you factor in all the various shipping, freight forwarding, customs, and registration fees. This car is already legally imported and ready to be enjoyed by the next owner.

If you are searching for a good quality Cinquecento, this car is definitely worth a look.

$9500 (or possible trade for other interesting classic car or vintage motorcycle).
