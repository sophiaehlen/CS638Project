1960 VESPA 400 Micro Car -- Rare Find for Microcar collectors

This 1960 Vespa 400 is an all original example in perfect running order. Original paint has some patina and a few small blemishes, but still looks terrific. No rust, no dents, very straight and solid body and trim. The interior is in very good condition and everything works as expected. Mechanically A1, starts readily and runs great. Brakes, trans, clutch and suspension all in top condition and maintenance is up to date. Odometer shows only 7970 kilometers - believed to be accurate.

Super fun to drive, quite reliable, simple to maintain and just the coolest micro ever. Extremely rare in this all original, low mileage, excellent condition. A unique addition to any collection or a very cute, attention getter for promotional purposes.

Introduced in 1957 the VESPA 400 was an innovative solution to the needs for compact and economical transportation in Europe's micro car marketplace.

The Vespa car was designed and engineered by Piaggio in Italy but manufactured in France.

Designed with aeronautical priorities and engineering sensibility, the Vespa 400 was a very compact, ultra light urban runabout with a very roomy interior and a slick rollback convertible top.

Twin bucket seats are augmented by a rear compartment that can be fitted with a cushion for kid-sized back seats or used for cargo.

Overall length is a trim 112" on a 67" wheelbase, curb weight is under 850 lbs.

The 400 features a twin cylinder, 393cc two-stroke twin cylinder engine with semi-automatic oil injection. Rated at 14 HP with a 3 speed manual transmission, performance is modest but adequate for the city. Top speed is 50 - 55 mph. Suspension is independent on all four wheels with coil sprigs, hydraulic shocks and a front roll bar. Brakes are drums at all four wheels. Fuel consumption is 45-50 mpg.

Contemporary road tests included a ringing endosement from Tom McCahill of Mechanix Illustrated, most popular auto reviewer of that era, who noted the smooth, comfortable ride quality and sharp handling . . .."Here is real fun. . . this car has a fantastic ride!".

The Vespa 400 was very sophisticated and well-engineered compared to the other microcars available, such as the BMW Isetta, and initial sales were very good. Piaggio addressed many needed improvements after launch and the 1960 model features all of the upgrades including automatic oil injection and opening door windows. Unfortunately for Piaggio, the European economy had improved making mini cars such as the Fiat 500 and BMC Morris Mini more popular in the marketplace. The microcar fad ended more quickly than expected, and the 400 was in production only until 1961.

It remains one of the most interesting of the microcars and a rare collectable today.

The car being offered is one of the finest, unrestored, all original Vespa 400s in the US.
